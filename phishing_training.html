The Basics

What is phishing?

Phishing is a form of social engineering; that means it's an attack that targets people, and not systems. Instead of breaking through technical defenses, an attacker tricks someone into voluntarily handing over something valuable: a password, financial details, or system access. The name is a deliberate pun on "fishing" — the attacker casts bait and waits for someone to bite.
